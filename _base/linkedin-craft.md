# LinkedIn craft — format, rhythm and storytelling

> Craft rules **specific to LinkedIn**, distilled from a book on positioning on the
> platform. The rules that generalize to all platforms live in `_base/voice.md`
> (short paragraphs, add value, sentence rhythm). What goes here is pure LinkedIn.
>
> Claude does NOT write this: these are instructions Claude passes to Blotato to write
> (see `post-writer`). The `post-grader` uses them as the LinkedIn "platform fit" checklist.

## 1. Mobile-first format (mandatory)
- **Paragraphs of 1–3 lines.** Never a dense block: people scroll and don't read.
- **Generous white space** between paragraphs. The air is part of the design.
- Sentences of varying length to create rhythm (see "ladders" below).
- One single concrete idea per post.

## 2. The "hidden text" trick (see more)
- LinkedIn collapses the post after ~140 chars (2 lines) with a "…see more".
- Put the **strong hook sentence up top**, then **2–3 blank lines**, and only then the body.
- Goal: make the collapsed part force the click. The first sentence has to stand on its own.
- The hook is still **50%** (see `hooks.md`): iterate it 3–5 times before the body.

## 3. Five rhythm hacks (combinable)
1. **Rule of 3** — lists of three points, positive/negative contrasts, comparisons.
2. **Ladders** — vary sentence length. Ascending (each one longer) or
   descending (each one shorter) to give cadence.
3. **Emoji combos** — group info with **functional** emojis (✅ do / ❌ don't,
   structure markers). In moderation, never decorative. See the emoji rule in `voice.md`.
4. **Numbered lists** — for processes or sequential steps.
5. **Anaphora (repetition)** — repeat a word at the start of several lines to create rhythm.
- Powerful combos: Rule of 3 + lists · Emoji combos + ladders · Anaphora + clear CTA.

## 4. Storytelling — 8-step structure (when the post is narrative)
Use when the angle is a story/confession/case, not for every post.
1. **Powerful hook** — a first line impossible to ignore.
2. **State the problem/challenge** — so the audience identifies with it.
3. **Protagonist** — motivations, fears, obstacles. Real vulnerability.
4. **Connect through emotion** — concrete moments: frustration, small wins.
5. **Action and solution** — the concrete steps that were taken.
6. **Climax** — the moment of change; build tension.
7. **Clear resolution** — an achievement, lesson, or transformation.
8. **CTA** — invite reflection, comments, or action (just one).
- Guiding rules: **authenticity + emotion**. "People connect with people, not with perfection."

## 5. Hook formulas from the book (mapping to `viral-hooks`)
Use as a STRUCTURE, adapt the content to the brand's wedge. Equivalences:
- **Pain list** (`Number + Problem + Undesired result`) → Pain Point List / Specific Number.
- **Curiosity** (`Question + Problem`) → Question Hook.
- **Story / confession** → Vulnerable Story Hook.
- **Direct statement about a pain / contrarian** → Reframe / Contrarian / "Most People" Reverse.
- Principle: connect with a **real pain or desire**, don't chase hollow virality.

## 6. Add value and interaction (LinkedIn)
- Be a **change agent**: add value before selling. The post should lead to a point of
  reflection that motivates improving something.
- Share: tactics with concrete results, tools, stories of overcoming odds, frameworks.
- **Comments are worth more than likes** for initial reach → close with a polarizing
  question or "what would you add?" (see CTA per platform in `platform-specs.md`).
- Tag with judgment, never spam.

## Quick checklist before scheduling on LinkedIn
- [ ] Strong hook in the first line (stands alone, passes the 3-words test).
- [ ] 2–3 blank lines after the hook (hidden text).
- [ ] Paragraphs of 1–3 lines, with air between them.
- [ ] At least one rhythm hack applied (rule of 3 / ladder / list / anaphora).
- [ ] Emojis only functional and in moderation (or none).
- [ ] One idea, one CTA that seeks comments.
- [ ] No links in the body (they go in the first comment).
